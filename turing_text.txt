Alan Turing was born in London on 23 June 1912. He proposed the Turing machine, which is a fundamental concept in computer science. During World War II, Turing worked at Bletchley Park to break the Enigma code. He wrote the paper "On Computable Numbers" in 1936. Turing also introduced the Turing test in his 1950 paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence". He was appointed an Officer of the Order of the British Empire in 1946. Turing died in Wilmslow, Cheshire in 1954.
